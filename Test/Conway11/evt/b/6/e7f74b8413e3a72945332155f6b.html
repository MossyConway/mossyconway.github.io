        Obituary for Mrs. Alice E. Hannan
        ATTLEBORO – Alice E. Hannan, 98, of Attleboro passed away on Thursday, February 2, 2017 at the Golden Living Center, Attleboro. She was the wife of the late John R. Hannan who passed away on May 10, 2016.

        Born on January 2, 1919 in Attleboro, MA, she was a daughter of the late Damas and Addie (Sabourin) Jette.

        Alice was a lifelong resident of Attleboro and attended the Attleboro Public School system. Her beautiful voice was discovered in high school where she starred in the operettas. She was a communicant of St. John the Evangelist Church in Attleboro and had a deep faith.

        She worked from home for B & J Jewelry and also at the Attleboro Fabric Shop part time. Alice enjoyed Tole painting, reading, sewing, cooking and knitting. At one time she was a Cub Scout leader. She treasured spending time with her husband, John, of seventy-two years and with her family, known as “GG” to her grandchildren.

        Alice is survived by her two children, Patricia Clement and her husband Marc of New London, NH and Peter Hannan of Gilford, VT; her grandchildren, Lucinda Carlson and her husband Timothy of Sandy Hook, CT, Catherine Mulready and her husband Declan of Derry, NH, Marc Clement II of Grantham, NH and Sean Wolf of Schenectady, NY; great grandchildren, John, Marc and Anne Marie Carlson all of Sandy Hook, CT and Aidan and Patrick Mulready both of Derry, NH; also many nieces and nephews.

        She was the mother of the late John Hannan II and the late Kathleen Wolf and sister of the late Raymond Jette, Omer Jette, Orient Jette, Rene Jette, Theresa Jette, Gertrude Miller and Simone Arenburg.

        Relatives and friends are invited to attend a visitation on Saturday, February 11, from 9:30 to 10:30 a.m. in the Duffy-Poule Funeral Home, 20 Peck St., Attleboro, followed by a Mass of Christian Burial, at 11 a.m., in St. John the Evangelist Church, 133 North Main St., Attleboro. Burial will follow in St. John’s Cemetery, Attleboro.

        In lieu of flowers, donations in Alice’s name may be made to St. John the Evangelist Church, One St. John’s Place, Attleboro, MA 02703.
