        DEATH Of ARCHDEACON HALPIN, P.P, V.G, RATHKEALE

        We regret to accounce the death of the Ven
        Archdeacon John Halpin, P P, V G, Rathkeale,
        which took place yesterday morning at the
        Parochial House in that town. The Rev gentle-
        man had been ailing for some time past. He
        was born at Cahirnorry, County Limerick, on the
        31st March, 1820, and received his early educa-
        tion at the classical school in Kilmallock. He
        afterwards became a student in the Limerick
        College, and finished his studies at Maynooth.
        On his ordination he was appointed curate of St
        John's Cathedral, by the late Most Rev
        Dr Ryan, and for a number of years
        lived with his lordship in the closest intimacy,
        while he was also most popular with his flock.
        He was one of the few remaining clergymen in
        the diocese who had to pass through the perilous
        days of cholera, and was remarkable for the
        heroic zeal with which he ministered to the poor
        plague-stricken patients in the lanes and alleys
        of St John's Parish. The late Most Rev Dr
        Butler promoted him to the pastoral charge of
        Kilfinane, and in 1876, on the death of the Rev
        Dr O'Shea, he was appointed P P of Rathkeale,
        and made Archdeacon and Vicar-General of the
        Diocese. He completed the tower of the new
        parish chapel and effected extensive improve-
        ments in the extension of the convent schools
        and grounds. Needless to say the Archdeacon
        was highly respected and esteemed by all
        classes, and his death is greatly regretted.
        Office and High Mass will be celebrted at Rath-
        keale on to-morrow at 11 o'clock. The Most Rev
        Dr O'Dwyer will preside.
