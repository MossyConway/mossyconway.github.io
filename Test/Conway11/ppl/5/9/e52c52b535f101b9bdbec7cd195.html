        ATTLEBORO – John R. Hannan, 96, of Attleboro passed away on Tuesday, May 10, 2016 at the Golden Living Center in Attleboro. He was the loving husband of Alice (Jette) Hannan of Attleboro.

        Born on August 16, 1919 in Attleboro, MA, he was a son of the late Thomas and Mary (Keane) Hannan.

        John was a lifelong resident of Attleboro. After graduating Attleboro High School, John pursued his education at Providence College where he received a B.S. in Accounting.

        John was employed as an accountant for several years working for the Narragansett Brewing Company, Apco Mossberg Co. in Attleboro, the Worcester Pressed Steel Company and Antaya Brothers, Inc. of Attleboro.

        He was a lifelong communicant of St. John the Evangelist Church in Attleboro and a member of Highland Country Club and Locust Valley Country Club both in Attleboro. John was an avid golfer and was the 1951 City Golf Champion of Attleboro. He treasured spending time with his wife of seventy-two years and his loving family.

        In addition to his wife, John is survived by his two children, Patricia Clement and her husband Marc of New London, NH and Peter Hannan of Gilford, VT; his grandchildren, Lucinda Carlson and her husband Timothy of Sandy Hook, CT, Catherine Mulready and her husband Declan of Derry, NH, Marc Clement II of Grantham, NH and Sean Wolf of Schenectady, NY; great grandchildren, John, Marc and Anne Marie Carlson all of Sandy Hook, CT and Aidan and Patrick Mulready both of Derry, NH; a sister Dorothy Cunningham of Greenwich, RI; also many nieces and nephews.

        John was the father of the late John Hannan II and the late Kathleen Wolf and brother of the late Thomas and William Hannan and Mary Brown.

        Relatives and friends are invited to attend a visitation in the Duffy-Poule Funeral Home, 20 Peck St., Attleboro, followed by a Mass of Christian Burial, in St. John the Evangelist Church, 133 North Main St., Attleboro. Burial will follow in St. John’s Cemetery, Attleboro MA.
