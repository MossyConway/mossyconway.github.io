        Obituary

        Helen R. Hannan Lepper, 98 of Attleboro, a loving mother, grandmother and great-grandmother, passed away peacefully surrounded by her family on Friday, January 21st at the LifeCare Center of Attleboro. She was the beloved wife of the late Thomas J. Hannan Jr. and the late John E. Lepper.

        Born Helen R. Phelan in Attleboro on February 12, 1912 Helen was the daughter of the late John and Louise (Gaffney) Phelan. She was raised and educated in Attleboro and was a graduate of Attleboro High School.

        Mrs. Lepper was employed as a Social Worker at the Attleboro Department of Public Welfare for 25 years before retiring in 1977. Mrs. Lepper was an avid reader and enjoyed spending time with her family, especially her great grandchildren.

        Mrs. Lepper was the mother of the late Thomas P. Hannan and is survived by a son Kevin Hannan and his wife Jean of Attleboro; three stepsons, John Lepper and Robert Lepper of Attleboro and Alan Lepper of Denver Colorado, four grandchildren, Deborah McCarthy of North Attleboro, Wende LeGacy and her husband James of North Attleboro, Susan White and her husband Martin of Walpole, Sean Hannan and his wife Panita of Attleboro, and seven great grandchildren, Paul, Hannah and Kyle McCarthy, Tyler and Brenna LeGacy, and Brian and Molly White.

        She was the sister of the late Eloise Phelan and the late Christine Hannon

        A funeral will be conducted on Tuesday, January 25th at 10 AM from the Duffy-Poule Funeral Home, 20 Peck St. Attleboro followed by a Mass of Christian Burial at 11 AM in St. John the Evangelist Church. 133 North Main Street, Attleboro, celebrated by Rev. Richard Roy, Pastor. Mrs. Lepper will be laid to rest in St. John's Cemetery, West Street, Attleboro. Calling Hours are respectfully omitted.
