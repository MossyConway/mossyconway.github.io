CLAREMORRIS PARISH RECORDS

The LDS film number for the Claremorris parish record is #1279207.

The Catholic parish of Claremorris is in the civil parish of Kilcolman and was also called by that name. There was another civil parish named Kilcolman, which was partly in the Mayo barony of Costello, near Ballaghadereen and Castlemore, and partly in counties Sligo and Roscommon. The two Kilcolmans should not be confused.

These records contain the marriage records for the years 1806-1890, and baptism records, 1835-1912.

These records are not complete, and many errors exist due to the very poor quality of the film. Many of the entries were not transcribed as they are illegible. I interpreted the handwriting to the best of my ability, and many errors were undoubtedly made. When searching the records, remember that many times the original transcriber spelled the names phonetically, and are not necessarily spelled as you may think. Many of the surnames had multiple variant spellings, so is best to search all variations. Therefore, you should use this information as a guide only and if you think you find an ancestor, you should always refer back to the film itself.

For the Marriages, “-pt-“ and the names mentioned
after this, are the witnesses to the marriages. In the actual records it
states “Pres.” meaning present at the wedding. For the Baptisms, I have been
using “-sp-“ meaning sponsors or Godparents.
