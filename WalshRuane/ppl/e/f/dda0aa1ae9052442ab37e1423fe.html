Event Note

“Michael took his family and belongings from Cossallagh to Logboy in 1908. The ‘Congested Districts Board’ acquired the Landlord’s estate, divided it, erected boundary fences, built a house and barn and allocated which of the nine families went where. The Clearys did better than most in so far as they were allotted a two-story house and a two-story barn and, arguably, the most arable land. That part of the landlord’s estate had been left idle for some years and had become overgrown with thistles, gorse, briars and scrub. With none of our modern machinery in existence on small farms away back then, I imagine, the Clearys and the other families had years of back-breaking hard labor ahead of them as they struggled to improve their holdings.” - Tom Cleary

Narrative

“Michael was good looking with his moustache and black bowler hat. He was bi-lingual and could read and write. The old people would gather periodically to listen to him read the newspaper for them so I was told). His mother was a Rattigan and he married a Rattigan but I presume they were not blood relatives. His wife came from a large family in Musicfield” - Tom Cleary

Narrative

At the time of the Griffith Valuation in 1856, Martin, the father of Michael , shared 89 acres of land in Lot #8 of Cossallagh with a Patrick Cleary who is proposed to have been Patrick Cleary Sr. On the basis of relative amount of taxes paid, Martin controlled about 1/3 of Lot #8a. Both Martin and Patrick had a house of their own. Martin held this land until his death in 1892, at which time it passed to his son, Michael. Michael and his family remained in Cossallagh until they moved to Lugboy Demense (see below) by 1911, at which time the land was distributed to unrelated parties.

In 1877 Martin also took over James Cleary’s lease of jointly-held lots 1 and 2 in Cossallagh and held them until his death.

Michael also held from 1877 to circa 1911 a share in lots 1 and 2 of Cossallagh formerly owned by his father, Martin.

Narrative

The land revision dated 1898 showed that Michael had greatly increased the value of their buildings from 5 shillings to 1 Pound 5 shillings. In an update to the Land Valuations from approx. 1912 it was clear that James Cleary, who had previously shared lot #8 with Michael, had taken advantage of the shuffling of land associated with the Irish land ownership revisions to incorporate into his ownership the fine building which Michael had erected.

      • Page: Lurgan Lot 1a from John Cleary of Lurgan to Michael Cleary of Logboy
      • Source text:

        The original GV for Lurgan showed Lot 1a of 41 acres owned by John Cleary and leased from Charles Boyd (the northern lot shown in yellow in the graphic above).  During the interval 1883-1898 the name in the Lurgan GV Revision remains John Cleary. John Cleary died in 1890 but the lot remained in the name of a John Cleary, probably his son, John Cleary Jr. b.1869. John’s Jr.’s death date and its potential interaction with the change in Lot 1a ownership is not known.  The first revision to this lot in the GV land records occurred in 1906-8 at which time it was changed to Michael Cleary, the son of Martin of Cossallagh and John Cleary’s purported nephew.  At this same revision the designation “house” was lined through, indicating that the house was in ruins or disuse.

      • Page: Lurgan Lot 1a from Michael Cleary to John "Blackjack" Cleary
      • Source text:

        In 1911, consistent with the timing of Michael Cleary’s move to Lugboy Demense, Lot 1a owner was changed to John Cleary, actually John (Blackjack) Cleary of Cossallagh; the property description remained the same but no house was listed.  Later, John (Blackjack) purchased Lot 1a from the Land Commission. In the GV revisions the lot remained in the name of John Cleary through 1934 at which time the GV revision method terminated. John (Blackjack) Cleary had died in 1929, but the land passed over time to his son John (Jackie) Cleary, thus the name on the Land Revisions, John Cleary, remained the same.
