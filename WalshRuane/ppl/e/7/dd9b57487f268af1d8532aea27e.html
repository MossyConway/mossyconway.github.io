Narrative

Catherine Delaney was reported by the grandson of Thomas Cleary as the daughter of Thaddy Delaney and Bridget Godfrey, but there is no documented evidence available.

The Buildings summary of the 1901 census indicates there were only two families living in Ballymangan, but that Catherine and her family were occupying the only large house in the township. She owned one lot and half of another, the total comprising about 230 acres. The other half of a lot was owned by John Heany (later by Bridget, his widow) and held about 52 acres. Judged by the building details, Catherine’s house was large: a stone dwelling with slate roof, 5 or 6 rooms, 6 windows in front of the house and 7 out-buildings. The old house is still standing is currently undergoing renovation. The census also shows that Catherine Cleary was the owner of the land, not a lessor.

In the old cemetery at Kilmaine, there was a marker noted for Thomas Cleary, d. 20 Jan 1891, 63 years old, erected by his son, Rev. T. (Timothy) D. Cleary.

The Death Record for Catherine was registered in the District of Hollymount in the Civil Parish of Ballinrobe. She died on Dec. 30, 1901, although the death is listed in the 1902 data. She died in Ballymangan, widow of a farmer, age 62 years, implying birth ~1840. Her death was witnessed by Patrick Cleary, her son. She is buried with her husband, Thomas, in the old cemetery at Kilmaine.
