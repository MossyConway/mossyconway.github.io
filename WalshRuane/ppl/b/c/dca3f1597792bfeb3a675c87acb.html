Narrative

There were a number of Cleary landholders in Cossallagh and Lurgan in the 1800s. The fact they lived in the same area, and that some of them shared lands and traded lands with each other indicates that those landholders may have been related, but the property records for the most part do not say what relationship, if any, existed between these landholders.

Evidence of the following relationships between the landholders has been found:

James and Martin, brothers – A funeral report of James’ son James (b1862) named Martin Cleary’s grandson Michael as a cousin of James (b1862). Specifically they were first-cousins-once-removed.

James and Patrick (b1808), brothers – Patrick’s death registration informant was his brother James Cleary of Cossallagh.

Patrick (b1780) and Thomas, father and son – Thomas Cleary was noted in a comment in the 1860-61 Cossallagh land revisions as the son of Patrick Cleary.

This tree speculates that Patrick (b1780) was also the father of Patrick (b ~1808), Martin (b ~1812) and James (b ~1821), but both Patrick (b1780) and Thomas are marked [Speculative] because there is no direct evidence of their connection to this tree.

[Evidence: ]
The only Cleary shown in the Tithe Applotment of 1833 for the Lurgan-Cossallagh area is a Pat Cleary & Co, landholder in Cusalough (Cossallagh), Parish of Bekan (later listed as being in the Parish of Annagh). Pat Cleary is listed as occupying land in “Cusalough, East and West”, totalling 41 acres. This description aligns with the size and location of Lots 1 and 2 in Griffith’s Valuation in 1856, occuppied jointly by James Cleary and two others. Other than the Pat Cleary noted, there were no other Clearys listed in the civil parishes of Bekan and Annagh.

The holding was listed at 41 acres 2 perches and 13 rods of arable bottomland, with comment “bog and road”. In 1833, the Patrick Cleary b. 1808 , the purported son of Patrick Sr. would have been only 25 years old and thus doubtful as lessee of such a sizable property. The probable owner would have been a Patrick Sr. The several other Cleary parties listed are all proposed as potential children of Patrick Cleary Sr. The following text will develop the basis for that logic.

The designation of “& Co.” to both Michael Murphy and Pat Cleary Tithe listings meant that they were standing (responsible for paying) the poor law tax for other unlisted parties. In an 1851 Summary of Tenants in Cossallagh, the primary holders remain Murphy and Cleary. The Clearys listed leases included line items for a Patrick, Patrick, Pat & Martin, Martin and James, all holding year-to-year leases. John Cleary is not shown at this time, but he does appear in the later Griffiths Valuation circa 1856 in the adjacent townland of Lurgan.

By the time of the Griffiths Valuation publication, the lots had been combined and re-numbered as shown in the GV extracts showing holdings by Patrick, Martin and James Cleary.

In 1856, the approximate time of the Griffith Valuation, Patrick held two thirds of 89 acres of land in Cossallagh jointly with Martin Cleary, below, as well as a house and out-office of his own.

In a property revision note in 1860-61, Patrick’s name as lessee was removed and the name of his son, Thomas, was substituted. See information on the page of Thomas Cleary 1828 - 1891. It is assumed that the elder Patrick died circa 1860.

Further informatin supporting this proposal appears on the page of each propsoed child of Patrick Cleary Sr.

Narrative

The Death Record for Patrick showed that his death occurred on the indicated date and age in Cossallagh. It further notes that he was a bachelor and a landholder. He died of old age and there was no medical attendant at his death. The record indicated that the informant was James Cleary, his brother, who lived in Cossallagh. Thus there is a firm tie between this Patrick Cleary and James, his brother. Further, this Patrick b. 1808 could be the brother of Thomas Cleary 1828 - 1891. On the page of Patrick Cleary 1785 - 1860 it is explained that the Patrick Cleary b. 1808 would not have been the Patrick noted as owner of part of lot 8a in Cossallagh in the Griffiths Valuation circa 1855.
