The Flanagan's of Brickens farmed the Cussolough and Lurgan land until Jackie was of an age to farm it himself. Valuation Office records report that Jackie's father's land in Cussolough was passed to 'Reps' in 1938 following his death in 1929. It was passed on again, most likely to Jackie, in 1949." - Niamh Flanagan

  1. Niamh Flanagan: Niamh Flanagan
      • Page: Mary Flanagan & family
      • Source text:

        In 1901 the 14 year old Mary Flanagan was living at home in Brickens. Mary had been a Nun in Cork for 3 or 4 years but was unwell and returned home. On her return home, her marriage to her father’s friend, the childless widower John Blackjack Cleary was arranged. She married at the relatively late age of 32 in about 1915. Her husband, John, was aged 69 at the time of their marriage. Mary was unwell with each pregnancy and died following childbirth on 8th September 1925. When Mary died, her mother, Kate Ronayne-Flanagan moved into John’s home in Cossallagh to look after John and Mary’s four children. Their four children were sent to live with different Flanagan cousins. Mary Delia, Tess and Miko all entered religious orders while Jackie remained on the family farm. Kate Flanagan’s husband, Michael Flanagan, followed her to John Cleary’s farm soon afterwards but he died within a year.

  4. South Mayo Family Research Centre & Jim Fritch: Cleary Family History Report
      • Page: Lurgan Lot 1a from Michael Cleary to John "Blackjack" Cleary
      • Source text:

        In 1911, consistent with the timing of Michael Cleary’s move to Lugboy Demense, Lot 1a owner was changed to John Cleary, actually John (Blackjack) Cleary of Cossallagh; the property description remained the same but no house was listed.  Later, John (Blackjack) purchased Lot 1a from the Land Commission. In the GV revisions the lot remained in the name of John Cleary through 1934 at which time the GV revision method terminated. John (Blackjack) Cleary had died in 1929, but the land passed over time to his son John (Jackie) Cleary, thus the name on the Land Revisions, John Cleary, remained the same.
