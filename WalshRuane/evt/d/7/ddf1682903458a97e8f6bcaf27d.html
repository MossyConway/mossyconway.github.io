  1. South Mayo Family Research Centre & Jim Fritch: Cleary Family History Report
      • Date: about 1875
      • Page: Thomas Cleary, the move to Ballymangan
      • Source text:

        Thomas and Catherine resided in Cossallagh until around 1875 when they moved to Ballymangan, Hollymount. The property revision documents for Cossallagh indicate 1877 as the date of lease transfer from Thomas to James Cleary, their relationship unknown, but they are putative brothers.

        The Irish Law Times of 1874 lists the same of land owned by John Nolan Ferrall in the townland of Ballymangan. Three of the lots listed are within the townland and comprise most of the townland area. The Land Revision records indicate that Thomas Cleary purchased one of the lots of about 50 acres. This would have been the property to which Thomas Cleary and his family moved around 1875. By 1901, the Land Revisions indicated that Thomas had purchased another of the lots, the largest, of 177 acres. The actual date of sale if not given, but would have been before Thomas’ death in 1891. This additional land purchase probably included the large house described in the 1901 Census.

        Ballymangan is described as being situated within two miles of the post-town of Hollymount and six miles from Claremorris. The land was described as of superior quality and well circumstanced as to roads.

      • Page: Thomas Cleary, land passed to son Patrick
      • Source text:

        With the death of Thomas in 1891, the land passed to his son Patrick. This was the time when the Land Act was appropriating with compensation the prior land owners and offering it to the former lessees at reasonable payback rates. The Cleary family, then led by Thomas’ son, Patrick, were successful in obtaining the option to purchase the land, except that held by Heaney, through to 1910.
