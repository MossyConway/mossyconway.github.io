  1. South Mayo Family Research Centre & Jim Fritch: Cleary Family History Report
      • Page: Lots 1 and 2 Cossallagh Patrick Cleary
      • Source text:

        The only Cleary shown in the Tithe Applotment of 1833 for the Lurgan-Cossallagh area is a Pat Cleary & Co, landholder in Cusalough (Cossallagh), Parish of Bekani (later listed as being in the Parish of Annagh). Pat Cleary is listed as occupying land in “Cusalough, East and West”, totalling 41 acres. This description aligns with the size and location of Lots 1 and 2 in Griffith’s Valuation in 1856, occuppied jointly by James Cleary and two others. Other than the Pat Cleary noted, there were no other Clearys listed in the civil parishes of Bekan and Annagh.

        The holding was listed at 41 acres 2 perches and 13 rods of arable bottomland, with comment “bog and road”. In 1833, the Patrick Cleary b. 1808 , the purported son of Patrick Sr. would have been only 25 years old and thus doubtful as lessee of such a sizable property.

        The designation of “& Co.” to both Michael Murphy and Pat Cleary Tithe listings meant that they were standing (responsible for paying) the poor law tax for other unlisted parties. In an 1851 Summary of Tenants in Cossallagh, the primary holders remain Murphy and Cleary. The Clearys listed leases included line items for a Patrick, Patrick, Pat & Martin, Martin and James, all holding year-to-year leases. John Cleary is not shown at this time, but he does appear in the later Griffiths Valuation circa 1856 in the adjacent townland of Lurgan.
