Property

Date estimated from 1850 to 1877
Place Cossallagh, Culnacleha, Mayo, Ireland
Description Lots 1 and 2

Narrative

The Griffith Valuation circa 1855 showed James Cleary to hold, in partnership with John McGee and John Burke, lots 1 and 2 of the townland of Cossallagh with lot 1a listed by his name.5 The data showed that James paid about 40% of the total land tax. James held this land lease until 1877 when the land transferred to Martin Cleary, probably Martin Cleary b. 1812

        DESCRIPTIVE PARTICULARS

        These lands are for the most part good tillage soil and part bottom, enable of great improvement by a small expenditure in draining, and now affording good rough pasture, there is also a great quantity of veryapprovable bog, and a large proportion (estimated at eighty acres) of the demense is tastefully planted with useful and ornamental timber, varying from twenty to thirty years growth, which tends much to beautify and shelter the grounds. The house which is well sheltered and surrounded by neatly planted pleasure grounds, containing shrubs of the chiocest kin, can, by some outlay be made a commodious residence.
