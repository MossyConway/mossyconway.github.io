        1911 - Patrick Conway, a native of
        Tournafulla, was baptised on 18 February
        1877. He studied at Maynooth and was
        ordained there on 22 June 1902. After
        ordination he served in Bayyscullion (Derry
        diocese) until 1907, and after a brief spell
        at Donoughmore was appointed C.C. of
        Shanagolden on 18 September of that year.
        On 13 November 1911 he took up curacy
        at St. Munchin's parish until the following
        April when he was sent on a retreat to
        Mount Melleray due to health issues related
        to alcohol. He was subsequently appointed
        C.C. of Ardpatrick until December 1913
        when he was transferred to Manister. The
        follwoing June he returned to Ardpatrick
        until 24 March 1919 when he took up
        curacy at Croom. He retired from the
        priesthood in July 1922 and went to live
        at Belmont Park, Waterford, until his
        death on 12 OCtober 1933 aged 56. He
        was a relative of John Conway, C.C. of St.
        Munchin's parish for the period 1895 to
        1903.
